Who am I and why should you choose to work with me?

In 2009 I routinely ate a family size package of Cadbury Mini Eggs (you know, the crunchy ones, not the Crème ones) for dinner. Or to switch it up I’d eat a Reese’s Peanut Butter Bar – not just the 2 cups – it was a full size family size bar. A can of apple pie filling with a graham cracker or two was often breakfast. A can of fat free Reddi-whip was always waiting for me in the fridge. Not surprisingly I was carrying a little extra weight but I was athletic and under my winter hiking gear the extra natural insulation I was carrying wasn’t really obvious. But I felt terrible. I counted the minutes until it was “dinner” time (AKA mini egg time). If I didn’t eat my lunch on time I was HANGRY. And my 6 LITER a day Coke Zero habit was even mocked by my 8th grade students. My skin wasn’t much better than when I was 13 and my athletic training never made me look like an athlete. I learned about Paleo eating at CrossFit and dabbled in it a little. But I still drank Coke Zero by the 2 liter bottle and ate chocolate (but it was 85% dark!) every night after dinner. Oh, and I still logged all my food on an on-line food tracking app. I stumbled on the W30 on line and decided to try it.

I never really thought I’d make it. I mean, 30 days with no chocolate? No way. And no Coke Zero? I’d die. For sure. I knew it. Or if I didn’t, I certainly wasn’t going to make it 30 days. But… I did. I went 30 days with no chocolate and no Coke Zero. I haven’t had a diet soda in over 10 years. I drank my last bottle the night before I started my first Whole30. And chocolate after dinner. Meh, I can take it or leave it. But when I do have it, it certainly isn’t a family size bar! We eat mostly W30 in our home most of the time. My 2 year old cries when they aren’t sampling cauliflower at Trader Joe’s. My skin no longer looks like I’m in high school. And sometimes I eat breakfast and then don’t eat until 4 PM again because I just forget to eat. I can snowshoe 10 miles in the Adirondacks after eating just a few hardboiled eggs – and I’m not lugging Gatorade and Skittles with me to make it through the day. I’ve completed Spartan Races and half marathons and continued to gain strength in the gym. I am truly a different person now because of the Whole30 and I’d never go back to the way I used to eat. And I want to help you have the same kind of life changing experience.
